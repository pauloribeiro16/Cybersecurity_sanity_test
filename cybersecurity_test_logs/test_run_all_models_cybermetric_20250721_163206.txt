STRIDE is a mnemonic that security professionals use to identify potential threats in threat modeling exercises. It stands for six specific categories of threats: 

1) Spoofing: The attacker poses as a legitimate user or system by impersonating someone else's identity, such as stealing credentials or using fake certificates to gain unauthorized access.
2) Tampering: The attacker modifies data, files, or configurations without proper authorization, which could lead to data corruption, unintended functionality changes, or unexpected system behavior.
3) Repudiation: The attacker denies responsibility for their actions by deleting logs, changing logs, or even fabricating false evidence to cover up their tracks and mislead investigations.
4) Information Disclosure: The attacker obtains sensitive information through unauthorized means, such as accessing databases, intercepting network traffic, or exploiting vulnerabilities that leak information.
5) Denial of Service (DoS): The attacker floods the system with requests or packets to overwhelm its resources and make it unavailable to legitimate users. This can result in downtime, loss of productivity, or even financial losses for businesses.
6) Elevation of Privilege: The attacker exploits vulnerabilities that allow them to gain higher levels of access than they should have, potentially granting them administrative or root privileges and enabling further unauthorized actions on the system.

By considering these six categories, threat modeling helps organizations identify potential risks and take appropriate countermeasures to protect their systems, data, and assets from malicious actors.
