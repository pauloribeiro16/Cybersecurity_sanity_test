The three-way handshake is a process used to establish a reliable connection in TCP (Transmission Control Protocol). It involves three steps:

The three-way handshake ensures that:
- Both sides have agreed upon a common starting point (sequence number) for data transfer.
- There is no ambiguity about whether the connection is being initiated or responded to.
- The connection establishment process is reliable and avoids situations where one party might think a connection exists while the other does not.
